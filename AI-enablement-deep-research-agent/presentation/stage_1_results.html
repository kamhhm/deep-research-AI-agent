Pass 1

Tavily Web Search

For each company, Tavily searches the web using only the company name. This is deliberate: if a company can't be found by name alone, the deep research API (which also searches by name) won't find it either.

Design Decisions

Tavily API
Query format "Find information on the company {name}"
Search depth Advanced — returns reranked, higher-relevance results (2 credits/search)
Max results 5 results per company
Chunks per source 3 — multiple snippets per source for richer context
Include answer Advanced — Tavily's LLM generates a detailed company summary from search results
Website check HEAD request to homepage URL (alive/dead status)
Cost ~$0.011 per company × 44,387 = $500 total

Why name-only queries?

We tested multiple query strategies (including URLs, descriptions, and AI-focused keywords). Name-only queries serve a dual purpose: they retrieve general company information and act as a findability test. If a company has a generic name like "Den" or "Way", the search returns unrelated results — which tells GPT this company is not researchable.

Pass 2

GPT-5-nano Classification

GPT receives the Tavily results alongside the Crunchbase profile and performs two assessments: how much information is available online (presence), and whether a deep research call is worth the cost (priority).

Classification Logic

GPT-5-nano

The core logic is a consistency check: GPT compares Tavily search results against the Crunchbase profile. If results describe a different entity (common with generic names), research priority drops to 0-1.

For scores 4-5, the classifier also looks for AI/tech-forward signals in the search results — mentions of automation, AI tools, modern engineering, or operating in a space where AI adoption is common. This applies to any industry.

Research Priority Score (0-5)

Score Criteria Action
5 Exact match + excellent presence + direct AI/tech-forward signals. Deep research will definitely yield findings. Deep research
4 Exact match + strong presence + company appears tech-forward or innovative. Deep research potentially yields findings. Deep research
3 Exact match + decent presence + no particular tech signal. Unlikely to yield findings, but worth a shot. Deep research
2 Partial match, weak presence. Some footprint exists but not worth the cost. Skip
1 Results mostly describe unrelated entities. Barely any signal. Skip
0 No results, or entirely about unrelated entities. Not researchable. Skip

Key design decisions

4 few-shot examples anchor the model: score 5 (Sardine, fintech with AI signals), score 4 (Paramify, automation SaaS), score 2 (GreenLeaf, small traditional consulting), score 0 (Den, unrelated results).

Conservative bias: The prompt explicitly states that GenAI adoption evidence is "extremely rare" and that "most companies should score 0-2."

USE vs BUILD distinction: We want companies that adopt AI tools in their operations, not companies that build or sell AI products.
